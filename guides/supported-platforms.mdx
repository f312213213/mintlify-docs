JobBeacon supports the seven most common applicant tracking systems (ATS). Together they cover most of the companies you'd realistically want to watch.

If a company uses one of these, JobBeacon can watch it. If they roll their own custom careers page, JobBeacon can't (yet).

## Coverage caveats

Most boards work the same way, but a few have quirks worth knowing.

### Workday

Workday powers most large enterprises, and JobBeacon supports it — but with two caveats:

- **Multi-location jobs show "N Locations"** in the listing. Workday's listing API doesn't include the actual location strings for jobs posted to multiple cities. JobBeacon shows "N Locations" in the table and lazy-loads the real list when you hover.
- **Filters and unspecified locations.** Because Workday hides the per-location strings, location filters can't match those jobs directly. If you want them included, turn on **Include unspecified locations** in the company's filter settings.
- **Very large boards are capped.** JobBeacon tracks the most recent 1,000 roles per Workday board. The company page shows "Monitoring the 1,000 most recent of N listings" when capped.

### Eightfold

Same listing cap as Workday: the most recent 1,000 roles per board. Most companies don't approach this.

### Greenhouse, Ashby, Lever, SmartRecruiters, BambooHR

No caps. JobBeacon pulls every active role on the board.

## What JobBeacon does *not* watch

- **Custom in-house career pages.** If a company's careers page isn't backed by one of the boards above, JobBeacon can't extract structured job data from it. We don't run a headless browser to scrape arbitrary HTML.
- **LinkedIn, Indeed, or other aggregators.** JobBeacon goes to the source — the company itself.
- **Internal job boards** (e.g., the ones gated behind a company login).

If a company you care about isn't supported, [let us know](mailto:support@jobbeacon.app). We add platforms based on demand.
